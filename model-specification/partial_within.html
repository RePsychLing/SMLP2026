Partially-within subjects designs

Authors

Phillip Alday

Reinhold Kliegl

Published

2026-06-21

After working through this page you will be able to:

  • distinguish within-unit from between-unit factors and identify a partially-within design;
  • simulate data for such a design with MixedModelsSim;
  • specify the random-effects structure appropriate to crossed subjects and items.
NoteBefore you start

Prerequisites: Analysis of the sleepstudy data.

Datasets used: simulated data (no external dataset).

Begin by loading the packages to be used.

Code 
design_partial = filter(design) do row
    subj = parse(Int, row.subj[2:end])
    item = parse(Int, row.item[2:end])
    # for even-numbered subjects, we keep all conditions
    # for odd-numbered subjects, we keep only the two "odd" items,
    # i.e. the first and last conditions
    return iseven(subj) || isodd(item)
end

1 Exercises

  1. Classify the factors. For the design simulated here, which experimental factors are manipulated within subjects, and which are within items? Why does that distinction determine where each factor can appear in the random-effects structure?
NoteSolution

A factor can have a by-subject random slope only if it varies within subjects (each subject sees more than one level); likewise a by-item random slope requires the factor to vary within items. A factor that is between subjects (each subject sees only one level) cannot have a by-subject random slope — there is no within-subject variation for the slope to capture. In a partially-within design some factors are within one grouping factor but between the other, which is exactly what shapes the maximal random-effects structure.

  1. Change the design size. Re-run the simulation with more items per subject. How does increasing n_item affect your ability to estimate the by-item random effects?
NoteSolution

With very few items the by-item variance components and any item-level correlations are poorly estimated and the fit is prone to singularity; increasing n_item gives the model more information per item-level effect and generally stabilizes those estimates. Try n_item = 3, 10, 30 and compare issingular and the estimated item variance.
